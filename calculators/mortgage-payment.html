How the Mortgage Payment Calculator Works

Formula Used

M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1]

Where:

  • M = Monthly Payment
  • P = Principal Loan Amount
  • r = Monthly Interest Rate
  • n = Total Number of Payments

What's Included

  • Principal & Interest: The core loan payment
  • Property Taxes: Annual taxes divided by 12
  • Home Insurance: Annual premium divided by 12
  • PMI: Private Mortgage Insurance (if applicable)

Understanding Your Results

This calculator provides your total monthly housing payment, often called PITI (Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance). The principal and interest portion remains constant throughout your loan, while property taxes and insurance may change annually. PMI is typically required when your down payment is less than 20% of the home's value and can be removed once you reach 20% equity.
